Moqueca

Description

Moqueca is a traditional Brazilian fish stew. It's made with fish, coconut milk, tomatoes, onions, garlic, lime juice, and coriander. This dish originates from the state of Bahia and is known for its rich, creamy texture and complex flavors. It's typically served with rice and is perfect for seafood lovers.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds firm white fish fillets (like cod or halibut), cut into chunks
  • 3 tablespoons lime juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 cups chopped tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil (dendÃª oil)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix the fish chunks with lime juice, 1 clove of minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Let it marinate for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the coconut oil over medium heat. Add the onion and remaining garlic, cooking until softened.
  3. Add the bell peppers and cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook for 5 more minutes.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  6. Add the marinated fish and cook for about 10 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.
  7. Stir in the cilantro and palm oil.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot with rice.
