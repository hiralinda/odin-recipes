Feijoada

Description

Feijoada is Brazil's national dish, a hearty stew made with black beans and various cuts of pork. It's traditionally served with rice, collard greens, farofa (toasted manioc flour), and orange slices. This dish is perfect for large gatherings and is usually enjoyed on weekends.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dried black beans
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 pound slab bacon, diced
  • 1 pound linguiça sausage, sliced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 bay leaves
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Soak the black beans in cold water overnight. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the pork shoulder and bacon, cooking until browned.
  3. Add the linguiça sausage and cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened.
  5. Add the soaked beans, bay leaves, and enough water to cover everything by about 2 inches.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 2-3 hours, or until the beans are tender and the meat is falling apart.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with rice, collard greens, farofa, and orange slices.
