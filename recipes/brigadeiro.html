Brigadeiro

Description

Brigadeiro is a traditional Brazilian dessert, often described as a chocolate truffle. It's made from condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter, and is typically rolled into small balls and covered with chocolate sprinkles. This sweet treat is a staple at Brazilian birthday parties and other celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 1 (14 oz) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Chocolate sprinkles for coating

Steps

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the sweetened condensed milk, cocoa powder, and butter.
  2. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and easily pulls away from the bottom of the pan when you scrape it with your spoon (about 10-15 minutes).
  3. Remove from heat and transfer the mixture to a plate or bowl. Let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Once cooled, grease your hands with butter and roll the mixture into small balls (about 1 inch in diameter).
  5. Roll each ball in chocolate sprinkles to coat.
  6. Place the brigadeiros in small paper cups.
  7. Serve at room temperature or slightly chilled. Enjoy!
